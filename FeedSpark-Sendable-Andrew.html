Where the Real Problem Sits

FeedSpark does not have a sales problem. It has a visibility problem.

When an e-commerce director evaluates feed management vendors, they search Google. They check G2. They read comparison pages. They look for case studies with ROAS improvements and revenue metrics. At every step, they find Feedonomics, DataFeedWatch, and Channable. They do not find FeedSpark.

Feedonomics has published a page titled "FeedSpark best alternative" that ranks when someone searches your company name. That page defines FeedSpark to the market. And right now, FeedSpark has no published response. No comparison content. No review platform presence. No case studies with specific metrics.

The service quality is exceptional. 80% of support tickets closed within 24 hours. FeedHero is a genuinely differentiated platform. "AI plus rules plus experts" is a positioning statement no competitor can credibly claim. But positioning only works if the market can hear it.

Add River Island, Decathlon UK, and Gymshark and the figure approaches £500,000 to £800,000 — from six accounts that are making vendor decisions this quarter, with zero FeedSpark presence in any channel they use to build their shortlist. That revenue is flowing to Feedonomics and DataFeedWatch by default. Not because their service is better. Because they are visible and FeedSpark is not.
